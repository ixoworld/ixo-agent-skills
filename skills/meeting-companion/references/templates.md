# Follow-up Templates by Audience

Choose the template that matches the recipient. Keep the structure; adjust tone and length.

## Team recap (peers, informal)

```text
Subject: [Meeting] recap - [date]

Hey team,

Quick notes from today:

Decided:
- [decision]

Owners:
- [Owner]: [action] by [date]

Still open:
- [question] - [resolver] to follow up

Holler if I missed anything.
[sender]
```

## Manager update (concise, decision-led)

```text
Subject: [Meeting] - decisions and where I need you

Hi [Manager],

Recap of [meeting] on [date].

What we decided:
1. [decision] - rationale: [one line]
2. [decision] - rationale: [one line]

What I'm owning:
- [action] by [date]

Where I need a call from you:
- [open question or decision needed] - by [date]

Full notes attached if useful.
[sender]
```

## Client recap (formal, commitment-led)

```text
Subject: [Meeting name] - summary and next steps

Dear [Client],

Thank you for the discussion on [date]. To confirm what we agreed:

Decisions
- [decision]
- [decision]

Next steps
- [Our side]: [action] by [date]
- [Your side]: [action] by [date]

Open items
- [question we are tracking]

Please reply with any corrections by [date]. We will assume the above is accurate otherwise.

Best regards,
[sender]
```

## Board / executive update (one-screen, outcomes only)

```text
Subject: [Body] meeting - [date] - outcomes

Outcomes:
- [decision]
- [decision]

Risks raised:
- [risk] - owner: [name]

Next checkpoint: [date]

Full minutes available on request.
[sender]
```

## Tone calibration

- Default to the register of the source. If the meeting was casual, the recap is casual.
- One audience step up in formality is safe; two is jarring. A casual standup does not produce a board-style recap unless the user asks for one.
- Never add emojis unless the source used them.
