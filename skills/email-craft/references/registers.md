# Tone Registers - Side-by-Side

The same message, written in five registers. Use as calibration anchors.

## Situation

You need to push back on a deadline a stakeholder gave you.

## Formal (board, regulator, senior client)

```text
Subject: Vendor onboarding - timeline adjustment

Hello [name],

Thank you for sending the proposed timeline. Having reviewed it with the team, completing onboarding by 14 March is not feasible without compromising the security review. We can commit to 28 March with the full review intact, or to 14 March with a deferred review completing the following week.

Please let me know which option works for you. I can provide a more detailed schedule on request.

Kind regards,
[Sender]
```

## Warm-formal (regular client, senior peer)

```text
Subject: Onboarding timeline - quick reality check

Hi [name],

Thanks for sharing the timeline. Quick reality check: 14 March doesn't leave room for the security review without cutting corners we shouldn't cut. Two options:

1. Push to 28 March, full review intact.
2. Hit 14 March and finish the review the week after.

Which works on your side? Happy to walk through it on a call if easier.

Best,
[Sender]
```

## Warm-informal (peer, teammate)

```text
Subject: 14 March is tight - let me know

Hey [name],

14 March is tight - security review needs the extra two weeks. Can we land on 28 March, or do you want us to ship on time and finish the review after?

Let me know what works.

[Sender]
```

## Direct (report to manager, urgent)

```text
Subject: Need a call on the 14 March deadline

Hi [name],

Quick one: 14 March deadline conflicts with the security review. Two options - push to 28th, or ship on time and review after. I'd lean push, given the risk profile, but it's your call. Can you reply today?

[Sender]
```

## Cold-outreach (stranger, first contact)

```text
Subject: Quick question on timeline expectations

Hi [name],

We're scoping the onboarding work you mentioned. Before we commit to 14 March, want to flag that the security review typically needs two extra weeks. Most of our clients land on either:

- A full 28-March completion, review intact, or
- A 14-March go-live with the review wrapping in the following week.

Either is workable on our side. Which fits your team's process better?

[Sender]
```

## Calibration rules

1. **Length scales with formality.** Formal emails earn more sentences; informal ones cut them.
2. **Subject line tells the recipient what kind of email this is.** "Decision needed by X", "FYI", "Quick reality check" - the subject sets the tone.
3. **Sign-off matters more than people think.** "Kind regards" → "Best" → "[name]" each shift the register one click down.
4. **Never mix two registers in one email.** Casual opener with formal close (or vice versa) reads as nervous.
