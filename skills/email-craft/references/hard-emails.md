# Hard Emails - Patterns for the Seven Hardest

Treat these as starting structures, not scripts. Adjust to the specific situation.

## 1. Saying no without burning the bridge

Structure: acknowledge the ask → state the no, briefly → give one real reason → offer one alternative or referral.

```text
Hi [name],

Thanks for thinking of me for [X]. I'm not going to be able to take this on - my [Q1 / current commitments / specific reason] mean I can't give it the attention it deserves.

If it's useful, [referral / smaller alternative / future timing]. Otherwise, I hope it goes well.

Best,
[Sender]
```

Watch-out: avoid "maybe in the future" unless you mean it. People will follow up.

## 2. Delivering bad news (project slip, change in scope, broken commitment)

Structure: name the bad news in the first line → take responsibility where it's yours → explain only what helps → state the path forward → invite response.

```text
Hi [name],

I'm writing with news that won't be welcome: [the bad news, plainly].

This happened because [one-line cause - no over-explaining]. I take responsibility for [the specific part that's on you].

What this means for you: [concrete impact]. What I'm doing about it: [concrete action with date].

I'd like to discuss the path forward on a call this week. [Two time options].

[Sender]
```

Watch-out: do not bury the bad news in paragraph 3. Recipients hate the slow reveal more than the news itself.

## 3. Following up on silence (1st nudge, 2nd nudge, last attempt)

**First nudge** (1-2 weeks later, no shame in the game):

```text
Hi [name],

Bumping this in case it got buried. No pressure - happy to keep waiting if you need more time.

[One-line summary of the original ask]

[Sender]
```

**Second nudge** (assume the silence is signal):

```text
Hi [name],

Following up once more on [original ask]. If timing has shifted on your end or this is no longer a priority, just let me know - I'll close the loop and stop nudging.

[Sender]
```

**Last attempt** (closing the door, warmly):

```text
Hi [name],

Going to close this thread on my side. If [the thing] becomes relevant again, you know where to find me. Wishing you well on [their world].

[Sender]
```

Three nudges is the ceiling. Past three reads as desperate or aggressive.

## 4. Chasing an overdue commitment from a peer or report

Structure: state the commitment as remembered → ask for the current status, not the excuse → invite the path forward.

```text
Hi [name],

Checking in on [the deliverable] - I had us landing on [date], which has passed. What's the current state, and what would help to get it across the line?

[Sender]
```

Watch-out: do not lead with "you said you would". Lead with "I had us landing on" - same fact, less defensive trigger.

## 5. Escalating cleanly (going over someone's head)

Structure: state the issue → state what you've already tried → state what you need now → CC the original person, do not blindside.

```text
Hi [escalation point],

CC'ing [original contact] for visibility.

I'm raising [the issue] because [why it needs a higher decision].

What I've already done: [list briefly]. What I'm asking from you: [the specific decision or unblock].

Happy to hop on a call today or tomorrow.

[Sender]
```

Watch-out: never escalate without CC'ing the original person. Going behind backs is what makes escalations toxic.

## 6. Resigning (or telling someone you're leaving)

Structure: short, gracious, dated, no rehashing of grievances.

```text
Hi [Manager],

I'm writing to let you know I'll be resigning from [role]. My last day will be [date - two weeks from today, or per your contract].

I'm grateful for [one or two genuine things]. I'd like to use the next two weeks to [transition plan].

Happy to chat anytime this week.

[Sender]
```

Watch-out: resignation emails get screenshotted. Write nothing you wouldn't want a future employer to see.

## 7. Asking for money (raise, refund, payment, fee increase)

Structure: lead with the ask, then the basis, then the response window.

```text
Hi [name],

I'd like to discuss [a salary increase to $X / a refund of $Y / a fee increase of Z%].

The basis: [the strongest one or two reasons - not five]. [Market data / scope change / track record - whichever is most relevant].

Could we discuss this [before X date / on our next call / in writing]?

[Sender]
```

Watch-out: do not justify before asking. Asking first, then justifying, signals confidence. The reverse signals you're talking yourself out of it.

## Final rule

For every hard email: read it back as the recipient. Would you reply, ignore, or escalate? If "ignore" or "escalate", redraft.
